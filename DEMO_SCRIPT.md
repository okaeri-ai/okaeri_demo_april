# Okaeri — Demo Script

Exact talk track for the 5-screen prototype demo. Use with the demo mode (click "demo" in the toolbar) or navigate manually.

**Total time:** 6-8 minutes
**Audience:** Investors, design partners, or anyone evaluating the product
**Setup:** Open [okaeridemoapril.vercel.app](https://okaeridemoapril.vercel.app) in Chrome. Use a large screen or projector. Consider presentation mode (Cmd+Shift+P) for larger text.

---

## Before You Start

1. Hard refresh (Cmd+Shift+R) to ensure clean state
2. If using demo mode: click "demo" — screens auto-advance with tooltips
3. If navigating manually: follow the script below and click the noted buttons
4. Keep the sidebar open for the first demo; collapse it (Cmd+\) if screen space is tight

---

## Screen 1: Home (60 seconds)

**Navigate to:** #d01-home
**What they see:** One dominant card with the #1 action, compact watch items below

**Say:**

> "This is what Okaeri looks like when you start your day. It's not a dashboard. It tells you one thing: the most important action you need to take right now."

**Point to the dominant card:**

> "Send materials to Sequoia before the 10am meeting. Committed March 18, not sent, meeting in 28 minutes. Okaeri knows this isn't just a task — it's a time-sensitive commitment that affects your biggest fundraising relationship."

**Point to "also watching" items:**

> "Everything else is tracked but sorted by urgency. Index outreach is overdue. Alex at Mnemix is cooling. The rate limit bug ticket is ready to auto-execute. None of these need you right now — that one does."

**Point to "if you do nothing":**

> "And this is the cost of inaction. Sequoia materials arrive late, Index slips to week 4, Mnemix goes cold."

**Click:** "open prep brief" on the Sequoia prep card

---

## Screen 2: Prep Brief (60-90 seconds)

**Navigate to:** #d08-prep-brief
**What they see:** Structured prep with 4 sections

**Say:**

> "Before the meeting, Okaeri builds a prep brief. Not a summary of past notes — a structured guide for what to do in this specific conversation."

**Point to "what matters":**

> "Three things that matter, each with a source. Sarah asked for ARR clarity — that's from Gmail. Sequoia closed a $400M AI fund — that's from Slack. Michael is a first meeting — that's from LinkedIn research. Every claim is grounded."

**Point to person columns:**

> "For each person, what they care about and what to avoid. And these pinned guidance blocks — those are operator notes I saved by voice. 'Lead with technical differentiation.' Okaeri uses this in every prep, every draft, every suggestion for Sarah."

**Point to the recommended opening and likely objection:**

> "It even suggests how to open the conversation and what pushback to expect."

**Click:** "start recording"

---

## Screen 3: Live Meeting (90-120 seconds — the wow moment)

**Navigate to:** #d03-live-meeting
**What they see:** Transcript left, steering rail + signals right

**Say:**

> "Now we're in the meeting. Three things are happening simultaneously."

**Point to transcript (left):**

> "Transcript is running. Watch Marcus — he's about to commit to the rate limit bug."

**Wait for Marcus to type word-by-word. Let them watch.**

> "See? Real-time extraction as people speak."

**Point to the steering rail (right, top):**

> "This is the meeting steering rail. It's tracking the meeting's structural health. Right now: decisions are partial, one owner is missing, two deadlines are vague."

**Point to the first steering alert:**

> "It's flagging: 'API follow-up discussed twice, no explicit owner.' And it suggests exactly what to say: 'Who owns this by Friday?'"

**Wait for voice signals to fade in:**

> "And these — this is the moat. Voice signals that text tools can't capture. Conviction from Priya — she's confident, auto-execute enabled. Hesitation from James — confidence lowered, needs confirmation instead. The system is listening to HOW people talk, not just what they say."

**Point to a held-back intervention:**

> "And look — Okaeri chose NOT to intervene here. Priya is handling the pricing discussion with confidence. Interrupting would undermine her flow. 72% of potential interventions are held back. That restraint is the product."

**Click:** "end meeting"

---

## Screen 4: Confirm & Execute (60-90 seconds)

**Navigate to:** #d04-confirm-execute
**What they see:** Cards grouped by confidence

**Say:**

> "After the meeting: four commitments extracted, organized by confidence."

**Point to the green section:**

> "These two are high confidence. Marcus explicitly committed with conviction. Priya confirmed clearly. Safe to auto-execute."

**Point to the amber section:**

> "This one is medium. James said 'next week' but Okaeri detected hesitation. It defaulted to Thursday but flagged it."

**Point to the uncertain card (c4):**

> "And this is the key moment. Okaeri isn't sure if it's 'proposal sent' or 'proposal reviewed.' Instead of guessing — which would be dangerous for a Salesforce update — it asks. One tap."

**Click one of the clarification options.**

> "Watch — the plan updates immediately. Confidence goes from amber to green. Trust for Salesforce adjusts. The system learned from this correction."

**Click "execute all":**

> "145 milliseconds between each execution. Real actions across Linear, Slack, Calendar, Salesforce."

**Watch the cascade and confetti.**

**Click:** "see all clear"

---

## Screen 5: All Clear (45-60 seconds)

**Navigate to:** #d05-all-clear
**What they see:** State change view, not just completion

**Say:**

> "This isn't a 'done' screen. It's a 'what changed' screen."

**Point to each section:**

> "Executed — four actions completed across four tools. Memory updated — Okaeri now knows Marcus owns the rate limit bug with P1 priority. Relationships updated — Sarah is strong after today, Alex is still cooling. Trust adjusted — Linear gets +1 confirmed action, Salesforce gets recalibrated after the correction."

**Point to the narrative:**

> "'Next thing Okaeri will watch for: the Andreessen follow-up. James's rollover rate suggests this may slip. Will nudge Wednesday.' The system is already thinking about what might go wrong next."

---

## Closing (30 seconds)

**Say:**

> "That's the core loop. Before, during, after. Every meeting. Getting smarter every time. The execution rate goes from about 60% to 97%. That's 12+ hours per month of recovered founder time."

> "The prototype has 79 screens showing every feature in depth. Happy to go deeper on trust, voice signals, relationship strategy, contradiction detection — whatever is most interesting to you."

## Tips

- Let the live meeting screen breathe. Don't talk over Marcus typing — let them see it happen.
- The correction flow on d04 is the highest-impact moment. Pause and let it land.
- If they're technical investors, spend more time on d46 (attribution) and d47 (change detection).
- If they're operators, spend more time on d63 (whisper) and d65 (how okaeri works).
- Dark mode (Ctrl+Shift+D) works well for projector demos.
- Collapse the sidebar (Cmd+\) if the screen feels crowded.
