investor follow-up
learned · 6 instances
when to use: after a positive or mixed investor meeting
default sequence
1 Send recap within 24 hours
2 Include one proof artifact matched to their interest signal
3 If technical diligence is the open question, offer CTO Loom
4 If no reply in 5 days, send one concise bump — not a new pitch
5 Do not send a second broad pitch without new signal
best for: Sarah Rhodes · Nina Park · investor intros
why suggested: this pattern repeated across your last 6 investor threads
design partner momentum
learned · 4 instances
when to use: after design partner or pilot conversations
default sequence
1 Identify the current blocker
2 Hold generic outreach until blocker is resolved
3 Once resolved, send proof-of-progress update
4 Propose one specific next step (walkthrough, pilot, or credentials)
5 If legal, infra, or procurement is blocking, surface the missing dependency
best for: Alex Chen · Resonance · Mnemix
why suggested: your strongest partner threads move when outreach includes concrete technical progress
decision to execution
learned · 8 instances
when to use: after internal decision-heavy meetings
default sequence
1 Confirm what was actually decided
2 Assign one owner per next step
3 Convert vague timing into specific dates
4 Flag blocked items that need another stakeholder
5 Generate follow-through actions immediately after the meeting
best for: Q2 roadmap sync · planning meetings · exec reviews
why suggested: these meetings create the most commitments and the most slippage when owners and dates stay implicit
why playbooks
