the 5 steering detectors
1
no explicit owner
detects: An action item or follow-up discussed without anyone explicitly saying 'I'll do it'
why it matters: Unowned actions have a 73% drop rate within 48 hours
default mode: nudge after 2nd mention
2
no explicit deadline
detects: 'Soon', 'next week', 'when we can' — vague time language
why it matters: Vague deadlines slip 38% more often than specific dates
default mode: nudge after detection
3
no decision after prolonged discussion
detects: 3+ minutes on one topic without a clear decision frame
why it matters: Topics discussed without decision frames get revisited in the next meeting 81% of the time
default mode: nudge at 3 min mark
4
wrong stakeholder missing
detects: Topic requires input from someone not in the room (legal, finance, specific team member)
why it matters: Decisions made without required stakeholders get reversed 45% of the time
default mode: flag, suggest dependency capture
5
too much time on status
detects: More than 40% of meeting time spent on status updates vs decisions/blockers
why it matters: Status-heavy meetings produce 60% fewer actionable commitments
default mode: nudge at 40% threshold
intervention modes
silent
Okaeri observes and updates internal state. No visible interventions during the meeting. All insights saved for debrief.
best for: sensitive meetings, new clients
standard
recommended
Sparse nudges for critical structural failures. 2-3 interventions per meeting max.
best for: regular team meetings
high-touch
Active steering with frequent nudges. Every structural gap flagged in real time.
best for: high-stakes meetings with known patterns
off
No meeting steering. Okaeri only extracts commitments.
for: meetings where you want minimal AI presence
meeting scoring
Every meeting is scored against 5 criteria:
did we decide?
tracks whether explicit decisions were framed and confirmed
did we assign?
tracks whether every action has a named owner
did we clarify?
tracks whether vague language was resolved
did we bring the right people?
tracks stakeholder presence vs topic requirements
did we spend time on the right thing?
tracks time allocation vs agenda
The meeting score updates in real time and is shown in the debrief.
what makes this different
okaeri
Most tools summarize the meeting afterward. Meeting steering changes the outcome before the meeting ends. That is the jump: from memory, to execution, to live operational control. The key is restraint — if Okaeri intervenes every 3 minutes, it becomes unusable. The default is 2-3 nudges per meeting, each explained, each actionable.
