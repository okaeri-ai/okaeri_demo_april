recording posture
Okaeri only records when explicitly started
There is no always-on listening. Recording begins when you press 'start recording' or say 'hey okaeri, listen.' The mic orb turns amber when active.
All participants are notified
When recording starts, Okaeri announces itself: 'This meeting is being captured by Okaeri for commitment tracking.' Participants can opt out.
Recording stops when anyone asks
Saying 'stop recording' or 'off the record' immediately pauses capture. Those segments are never processed.

what can be forgotten
Delete a specific meeting
All transcript, commitments, and extracted data from one meeting can be permanently deleted. Downstream state changes are preserved but source attribution is removed.
Delete a person's data
All data associated with a specific person (relationship history, attribution, voice patterns) can be purged.
Delete everything
Full account reset. All data, all patterns, all learned preferences. Okaeri starts from zero.

okaeri analysis
Okaeri's approach to privacy is simple: you control the microphone, you control the data, and nothing is stored that you can't delete. Voice-first means trust-first. If participants don't consent, Okaeri doesn't listen. If you want something forgotten, it's forgotten. This isn't just a feature — it's the foundation of earned autonomy.
