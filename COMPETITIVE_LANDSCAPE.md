# Okaeri — Competitive Landscape

## The Category

Meeting intelligence tools that capture, summarize, or act on meeting content.

## The Landscape

### Transcript-First Tools

**Otter.ai**
- What they do: Real-time transcription, summaries, action item extraction
- Strength: Established brand, integrations, free tier
- Gap: No execution. Action items are text, not completed tickets. No trust model, no attribution, no voice signal analysis.
- Okaeri advantage: We execute, not just extract. We earn trust per-tool, not per-feature.

**Fireflies.ai**
- What they do: Meeting recording, transcription, search across meetings
- Strength: Good search, CRM integration, team features
- Gap: Search is retrospective, not proactive. No real-time steering, no contradiction detection, no relationship strategy.
- Okaeri advantage: We're forward-looking, not backward-searching. We tell you what to do next, not just what happened.

**Fathom**
- What they do: Meeting recording with AI highlights and summaries
- Strength: Clean UX, fast clips, good Zoom integration
- Gap: Summary-focused, no execution layer, no commitment tracking over time
- Okaeri advantage: Summaries decay in value within 24 hours. Execution compounds.

**Granola**
- What they do: AI notepad for meetings, generates structured notes
- Strength: Elegant design, good at structured note generation
- Gap: Notes are the output, not action. No tool execution, no trust model, no voice analysis.
- Okaeri advantage: Notes are an intermediate artifact. Execution is the outcome.

**Tactiq**
- What they do: Real-time transcription with AI summaries for Google Meet/Zoom
- Strength: Browser-based, lightweight, quick summaries
- Gap: No execution, no state across meetings, no organizational memory
- Okaeri advantage: We build cumulative intelligence, not per-meeting summaries.

### Task/Execution Tools

**Linear, Asana, Jira**
- What they do: Task management and project tracking
- Relationship to Okaeri: These are execution targets, not competitors. Okaeri creates tickets in Linear, not replaces it.
- Gap: They don't know what was said in meetings. They only know what someone typed.
- Okaeri advantage: We bridge the gap between spoken commitment and tracked task.

### AI Assistants

**ChatGPT / Claude / Copilot**
- What they do: General-purpose AI assistants
- Relationship to Okaeri: Different category. General assistants answer questions. Okaeri executes operational workflows.
- Gap: No persistent memory of your meetings, no trust model, no tool execution, no voice-first interface
- Okaeri advantage: Domain-specific > general-purpose for execution workflows.

### CRM

**Salesforce, HubSpot**
- What they do: Contact and deal management
- Relationship to Okaeri: Execution targets. Okaeri updates Salesforce, not replaces it.
- Gap: Manual data entry. Nobody updates the CRM after every meeting.
- Okaeri advantage: Automatic CRM updates from spoken commitments with confidence scoring.

## The Moat Hierarchy

1. **Commodity:** Transcription (everyone has this)
2. **Emerging:** Action item extraction (most are adding this)
3. **Rare:** Tool execution with confidence scoring (Okaeri)
4. **Unique:** Earned autonomy + voice signal analysis + commitment attribution (Okaeri only)
5. **Defensible:** Organizational memory that compounds over time (Okaeri's long-term moat)

The further up this hierarchy, the harder it is to replicate. Transcription is a feature. Organizational memory is a platform.

## Why Not Just Add Execution to Otter?

Because execution without trust is dangerous. Otter could add a "create ticket" button. But:
- Would you trust it to auto-create tickets? Without confidence scoring?
- Would it know that Marcus says "I'll take it" with 92% follow-through but James says "next week" with 62%?
- Would it know to ask "proposal sent or proposal reviewed?" instead of guessing?
- Would it hold back from scheduling a meeting because it detected a calendar conflict?

Execution without judgment is just automation. Execution with earned trust is an operating partner.

## The Real Competition

The real competition is not another tool. It's the founder's own memory and their existing workflow:
- Scribbled notes on a legal pad
- "I'll remember to do that"
- A Slack message to yourself
- The CRM entry you'll do "later"

Okaeri wins by being less work than the current approach while producing better outcomes. The bar is low. The improvement is measurable.
