# Okaeri — Principles

Ten rules that govern every product decision.

---

**1. Judgment over tracking.**
Every screen should tell you what to do, not just what happened. A dashboard shows activity. A command surface shows the one action that matters right now.

**2. Restraint over noise.**
72% of potential interventions are held back. The system earns attention by being sparse and right, not frequent and hopeful. If Okaeri speaks, it should change the outcome.

**3. Evidence over assertion.**
Every claim has a source. Every trust level has data behind it. Every recommendation shows why. "Sarah responds to technical depth" is useful. "Sarah responds to technical depth — based on 3 positive replies to product updates and 0 responses to generic check-ins" is trustworthy.

**4. Correction over perfection.**
The system will be wrong. That's expected. What matters is: does it ask before acting on uncertainty? Does it learn from corrections? Does it get better every week? Showing a mistake handled well builds more trust than showing no mistakes.

**5. Voice over dashboard.**
If it can be heard, don't make them read it. Pre-meeting briefs are spoken. Post-meeting debriefs are spoken. Corrections are conversational. The dashboard exists for exploration, not as the primary interface.

**6. Earned over granted.**
Trust accumulates through consistent, transparent behavior. It starts at zero. It can regress. Every auto-executed action, every correct extraction, every accepted draft earns a small increment. Every rollback costs more than it took to earn.

**7. Cumulative over transactional.**
Every interaction makes the system smarter. A correction today improves extraction tomorrow. A voice note today shapes prep briefs next week. The product compounds — that's the moat.

**8. Strategy over status.**
"Alex Chen — 11 days, cooling" is status. "Alex responds to direct asks with technical proof. Wait for the bug fix, then send proof-of-progress within 24 hours" is strategy. Status tells you what happened. Strategy tells you what to do.

**9. Coherence over features.**
The trust logic, signal logic, and action logic must say the same thing. If voice signals lower confidence, that must change the execution bucket. If trust regresses, that must change permissions. Inconsistency in the model destroys credibility faster than missing features.

**10. Negative space is structural.**
Emptiness communicates "all clear." Silence means nothing needs you. An empty home screen is the product working perfectly. Don't fill space that should be empty. Don't add features that don't earn their place.
